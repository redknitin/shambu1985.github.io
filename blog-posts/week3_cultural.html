Cultural Blog: Thoughts on My Thinking Style

The Abstract Sequential Thinker

10th September 2014

Each one of us has a personal thinking style that organizes how we interact with other people, information, and problem solving. I believe understanding one’s personal thinking style is a good place to self-acceptance and the acceptance of others. After taking the personal thinking style quiz, and researching a bit about my thinking style, I found my personal thinking style to be more or less abstract sequential. The theoretical model created by Antony Gregorc helped me understand and figure out my personal thinking style.

I enjoy theory, precision, logic, abstract thought and tend to learn best by listening to lectures, independent research and following procedures. I generally give my best working on a particular task in a quiet environment and generally do not prefer to give up unless and until I end up with a satisfactory solution.

I prefer to take sufficient time to master a particular topic and do not believe in rushing through it. Currently this approach is working great in completing DBC’s weekly challenges. The best way to handle pressure is to be well equipped for it and act upon it wisely.

Referring through multiple resources to gain mastery over a particular topic has always been my method of learning. Also like an AS thinker, I prefer to work alone over the challenges and have never worked together in groups to accomplish a particular task. Personally I am a bit impatient person and tend to get easily distracted when things are not in my control. Learning experience at Dev Bootcamp has helped me become more patient. I also learnt the importance of pair programming, working together and be a part of the productive experience of learning, figuring out things together.

One other exciting thing about being at Dev Bootcamp is to see my own talent growing every week and me getting more and more inclined towards the growth mindset. Even if I am not so confident about a particular challenge, I tend to face the challenge wholeheartedly, learn from my setbacks, research more and try again.
